How it works

A marketplace built around real charging economics

This model uses distributed Level 2 charging with lower installation cost, easier hosting, and a strong fit for overnight and multi-hour charging.

🏠

1. Host installs a connected charger

A homeowner, apartment operator, or business buys a GridShare-ready station that meters energy, captures usage data, and connects to the marketplace.

🚗

2. Driver books a nearby charger

Drivers browse available chargers, compare rates and access windows, then reserve the best option for overnight, workday, or destination charging.

📊

3. Revenue is split automatically

The host is reimbursed for electricity used, the platform takes its fee, and the remaining margin is shared automatically. Hosts can track every session in a dashboard.

FAQ

Common questions

A better way to unlock cheaper, bookable, local charging using distributed host sites.

How is pricing set?

Pricing is based on host electricity cost, time-of-use windows, charger overhead, platform fees, and a host margin. This keeps rates predictable for drivers while protecting the host from losing money.

Why would a driver use this instead of public charging?

This model works well for cheaper, reservable neighborhood charging during multi-hour stays such as overnight, workday, destination, and apartment overflow charging. It complements fast charging rather than replacing it.

Who should host a charger?

The strongest sites are easy-access homes, apartments, small businesses, churches, schools, and underused lots where drivers already park for several hours.

Does every host make good money?

No. Earnings depend heavily on utilization, local power cost, and access quality. Results are strongest when the site is visible, bookable, and located near repeat local demand.
